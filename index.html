It is a period of operational disorder.

Multi-site enterprises are drowning in fractured systems. Siloed ERPs that refuse to communicate. Fragile spreadsheets traded over email. Receipts photographed on the fly. Handwritten production notes buried in filing cabinets.

For more than twenty years, ADAM REEP has dismantled this pattern.

At EDMC, he initiated a telecom-expense recovery program that recaptured $2.5M in annual recurring savings across a 129-campus footprint.

At MILLER, he turned paper into queryable data — an OCR-to-Trello pipeline that gave the field workload metrics for the first time — while migrating 1,332 cardholder profiles between competing vendors with zero recorded errors.

And in four days, he built a ten-module OCR + LLM document engine designed to address the exact operational chokepoints where growing businesses get trapped.

His method has hardened into a discipline:

Find the system of record.

Build it if it doesn't exist.

Force every tool to read from it.

AI at the edge. Automation at the core.

Eradicate the paperwork. Reinvest the hours.

Machines process. People solve.
